About Culdesac

Culdesac represents a bold experiment in American urban planning. Founded in 2018 by Ryan Johnson (CEO) and Jeff Berens (COO), the company aims to prove that car-free neighborhoods can succeed in the U.S. market.

Their flagship project, Culdesac Tempe, is the first car-free neighborhood built from scratch in the United States. This 17-acre development in Tempe, Arizona, accommodates over 1,000 residents and demonstrates their vision for sustainable urban living.

Their Approach to Urban Design

Community-First Design

Culdesac prioritizes shared spaces over private amenities, fostering neighbor interaction through courtyards and common areas.

Pedestrian-First Infrastructure

Every street is designed for walking and biking first, with cars as an afterthought - a radical departure from typical American suburban planning.

Sustainable Building Practices

The development incorporates renewable energy, water conservation, and eco-friendly materials to minimize environmental impact.

Alternative Mobility Solutions

Culdesac's approach to transportation challenges traditional American car dependency:

  • Bike Infrastructure: Comprehensive bike-share program with both electric and traditional bikes
  • Micro-mobility: Electric scooters for short-distance travel
  • Rideshare Integration: Partnerships with ride-sharing services for longer trips
  • Delivery Services: Same-day delivery for groceries and packages, reducing shopping trips
  • Public Transit: Strategic connections to existing public transportation networks

Innovation: This multi-modal approach proves that car-free living is viable in American suburbs.

Health & Wellness Integration

Culdesac leverages the walkable environment to promote healthier lifestyles:

  • Built-in Fitness: State-of-the-art equipment integrated into the community
  • Mindfulness Programs: Daily yoga and meditation classes
  • Personal Training: On-site fitness professionals
  • Outdoor Activities: Fitness areas that take advantage of the 55% open space
  • Wellness Education: Workshops on healthy living and sustainability

Design Philosophy: Physical activity becomes part of daily life through intentional urban design.

×

Community Dining Model

Culdesac's shared dining approach reduces individual consumption while building community:

  • Shared Kitchen: Commercial-grade facilities available to all residents
  • Community Hall: Central dining space for group meals and events
  • Outdoor Dining: Al fresco eating areas integrated with green spaces
  • Educational Programs: Cooking classes and nutrition workshops
  • Local Partnerships: Farm-to-table connections with regional suppliers

Social Impact: Shared dining spaces encourage neighbor interaction and reduce food waste.

Social Programming Strategy

Culdesac curates events specifically designed to foster community connections:

  • Local Markets: Weekly farmers markets supporting regional producers
  • Skill Sharing: Community workshops on sustainability and urban living
  • Cultural Events: Performances and art installations in public spaces
  • Outdoor Cinema: Movie nights utilizing the abundant outdoor areas
  • Seasonal Celebrations: Community-wide events that build neighborhood identity

Community Building: Intentional programming creates social bonds that traditional suburbs often lack.
